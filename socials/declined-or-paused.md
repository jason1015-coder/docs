# Declined or Paused

**When**: a Stage 3 decline lands, OR an in-progress project moves to `Paused`.

**Voice**: this is the post most collectives skip. The willingness to do them is a brand asset. Be specific, kind, and honest. No defensiveness, no false equivalence.

## Decline — short post

We considered **{Project Name}** and decided not to build it now.

The whitepaper had a real case. What it did not have was {specific reason — maintainer commitment, alignment with current priorities, a tractablev1.0 scope, etc}. The full rationale is on the whitepaper page.

The whitepaper stays in place. If the missing piece changes, the door is open.

→ {link to whitepaper, with decline note}

## Decline — long post

A whitepaper just hit `Declined` under the Nano Collective.

**{Project Name}** went through the same 30-day public review as every other proposal. The case for building was real, the thinking was sharp, and the review window produced substantive engagement.

The decline was on {one or two specific criteria that were not met}. Specifically: {one or two sentences on the actual reason}.

This is not a moral judgement on the proposal. Whitepapers get declined for the same reason any well-argued thing gets declined: the timing, the fit, the maintainer commitment, or the scope did not line up. The full rationale is recorded on the whitepaper page, dated, signed off by the core team.

The whitepaper itself stays where it is. It is a real piece of work and a real piece of the collective's thinking. After 30 days, it moves to the archive, with its URL preserved.

If something material changes — a new maintainer steps forward, a partner provides what was missing, the scope tightens — the proposer or anyone else can reopen the proposal. There is no penalty for coming back.

## Pause — short post

**{Project Name}** is paused.

It was approved to build {timeframe ago}, but {brief honest reason — proposer stepped back, scope hit a real blocker, maintainer capacity disappeared}.

The whitepaper stays in place. If anyone wants to pick it up, the door is open.
